Goal

Your have been chosen to lead a start-up that sells a software. The goal is to get the most market share before the time runs out, but beware the competition may be tough!

  Rules


During the competition 4 start-ups will try to get the most market share.
To sell your software you will have to recruit employees and give them orders.
The game stands for 100 turns unless a start-up reach 80% market share before


Chronology

Each turn following actions are performed:
  1. Recruitment : increasing or decreasing employees
  2. Pay : Your cash increases and all employees receive their pay (including new ones)
  3. Development: new features are developed (and new bugs appear)
  4. Sales : sellers sell your software, increasing your market share


Recruitment

Your can recruit different types of employees each turn:
  • Developers, developing new features and maintaining for your software
  • Sellers, selling your software increasing your market share
  • Managers, manage other employees so that they perform their task correctly
There are limitations:
  • You can recruit or fire maximum 1 manager each turn
  • |devsToHire| + |sellersToHire| ≤ 2 × managers where |n| means absolute value of n
  • employeeCount ≤ 10 × managers. Where employeeCount is your total number of developers and sellers


Employees affectations

Different affectations for your employees:
  • Developers : new features or maintenance
  • Sellers : unfilled market or try to steal market share from competitors
  • Managers : manage other employees
To work well, ratio between employees and managers should be 1 manager per 4 employees.
Employees outside this ratio will change their affectation randomly by:
  • doing the action you intend to
  • doing a different action (devs will switch from features to maintenance, sellers from unfilled market to competitive market)
  • doing nothing


Pay

Each turn your cash increases proportionally to your market share. cash = cash + marketShare in thousandth
Employees don't work for free, you will have to pay them each turn:
  • Developers : $10 for each
  • Sellers : $10 for each
  • Managers : $20 for each
For example: 5 developers, 3 seller, 2 managers will cost 5 × 10 + 3 × 10 + 2 × 20 = $120.
If you don't have enough cash, your employees will be fired one by one until you have enough money, starting by developers, then sellers, then managers.
In the previous example, if you had only $100 cash 2 developers would be fired.


Development

features developed increase by the number of developers affected to new features
bugs count decrease by the number of developers affected to maintenance and tests increase by the same number
New bugs appear. Indeed, for the next 9 rounds after a feature has been developed it has a chance to produce a bug
Pbug(t) = e^(-0.3 × t) × max(0, 1 - 0.25 * tests / features)
You can find at the end a table of 9 first values of e^(-0.3 × t)
Note: if you have 4 times more tests than features you can't have bugs anymore.
Note: tests will always increase by maintenance devs count, event if there is no bug.

Reputation
Even fixed, bugs damage your start-up reputation and make harder the work of your sellers.
Your reputation is calculated by:
reputation = 100 × features / (existingBugs × 3 + fixedBugs)


Sales

Market is always growing, all start-ups have their market reducing by 5%.
Phase 1 - Unfilled market:
Your start-up will get an unfilledMarketScore calculated by:
unfilledMarketScore = unfilledMarketSellers × reputation × features
Then your market share will increase by availableUnfilledMarket × unfilledMarkerScore / totalUnfilledMarketScore
Where availableUnfilledMarket = min(4% × playerCount, remainingUnfilledMarket)

Phase 2 - Competitive market
Your start-up will get a competitiveMarkerScore calculated by:
competitiveMarketScore = competitiveMarketSellers × reputation × features
Then all start-ups will fight once in a random order.

Fight example between start-up A and start-up B:
If reputationA is greater than reputationB
Then start-up A will steal market share from start-up B calculated by:
stolenMarketShare = min(0.8%, 0.4% × competitiveMarkerScoreA / competitiveMarkerScoreB)
Optional rule:
Instead of fighting everyone you can decide to focus on only one competitor in this case stolenMarket will be calculated by:
stolenMarketShare = (playerCount - 1) × min(0.8%, 0.4% × competitiveMarkerScoreA / competitiveMarkerScoreB)

 
Victory conditions
  • You reach 80% market share.
  • You have the most market share after 100 turns.
 
Lose conditions
  • Your program input is invalid.
  • An other start up reach 80% market share.
  • Your program doesn't provide an input.

  Note

e^(-0.3 × t) values:
  • t = 1 : 0.740
  • t = 2 : 0.549
  • t = 3 : 0.407
  • t = 4 : 0.301
  • t = 5 : 0.223
  • t = 6 : 0.165
  • t = 7 : 0.122
  • t = 8 : 0.091
  • t = 9 : 0.067

  Game input

Input for a turn

Line 1 : 10 integers id, playerCount, turn, cash, devs, sellers, managers, features, tests and bugs where:

  • id : your player id
  • playerCount : number of players
  • turn : number of turn since the beginning
  • cash : cash of your start-up
  • devs : developers employees of your start-up
  • sellers : sellers employees of your start-up
  • managers : managers employees of your start-up
  • features : features developed in your software
  • tests : tests developed in your software
  • bugs : bugs in your software

playerCount following lines : one line for each player. First line is for player 0, next one for player 1, etc. Each line contains 3 integers startUpId start-up id, marketShare start-up market share and reputation start-up reputation

Output for a turn
One line containing 5 or 6 integers separated by space devsToHire, sellersToHire, managersToHire, maintenanceDevs, competitiveSellers and targetId

Constraints for output values:

  • -1 ≤ managersToHire ≤ 1
  • |devsToHire| + |sellersToHire| ≤ 2 × managers where |n| means absolute value of n
  • 0 ≤ maintenanceDevs ≤ devs + devsToHire
  • 0 ≤ competitiveSellers ≤ sellers + sellersToHire
  • devs + devsToHire + sales + salesToHire ≤ 10 × managers
devsToHire: number of developers you want to hire for next round, it can be negative.
sellersToHire: number of sellers you want to hire for next round, it can be negative.
managersToHire: number of managers you want to hire for next round, it can be negative.
maintenanceDevs: number of your developers affected to fix bugs and increase tests
Beware tests number can be increased only if a feature already exists or has been developed during the turn
competitiveSellers: sellers that will try to steal market share from competitors
targetId must be a player id (different from your id)
