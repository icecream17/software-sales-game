Goal

Your have been chosen to lead a start-up that sells a software. The goal is to get the most market share before the time runs out, but beware the competition may be tough!

  Rules

During the competition4 start-ups will try to get the most market share.
To sell your software you will have to recruit employees and give them orders.

Your can recruit different types of employees:
  • Developers, developing new features and tests for your software and fixing bugs
  • Sellers, selling your software increasing your market share
  • Managers, helping you to control other employees so that they perform their task correctly

Pays rules
Each turn your cash increases proportionally to your market share in thousandth
Employees don't work for free, you will have to pay them each turn:
  • Developers : $10 for each
  • Sellers : $10 for each
  • Managers : $20 for each
For example: 5 developers, 3 seller, 2 managers will cost 5 × 10 + 3 × 10 + 2 × 20 = $120.
If you don't have enough cash your employees will be fired one by one until you have enough money, starting by developers, then sellers, then managers.
In the previous example if you had only $100 cash 2 developers would be fired.

Employees actions
Different type of actions can be performed by your employees:
  • Developers : they can either work on developing new features either work on fixing existing bugs and developing tests
  • Sellers : they can either sell for new client either try to steal clients for other start-ups (harder)
  • Managers : manage other employees and determine your recruitment capacity
But be careful! If you have less than 1 manager per 4 employees (developer or seller) they might do something else (A developer that was developing new features might decide by himself to debug instead, or to do nothing!)
For example if you have 60 employees and 10 managers, 40 employees will follow their order, each of 20 others has:
  • 34% chance to do the action you intend to
  • 33% chance to do a different action
  • 33% chance to do nothing

Recruitment capacity
You can recruit or fire 1 manager each turn, there is no maximum limit
You can recruit of fire each turn 2 × managers developers plus sellers
You can recruit a total of 10 × managers employees in your start-up, excluding managers

Chronologie of a turn
Each turn following actions are performed:
  1. Recruitment : increasing or decreasing employees
  2. Pay : Your cash increases and all employees receive their pay (including new ones)
  3. Development: new features are developed (and new bugs appear)
  4. Sales : sellers sell your software, increasing your market share

 
Victory conditions
  • You reach 80% market share.
  • You have the most market share after 100 turns.
 
Lose conditions
  • Your program input is invalid.
  • An other start up reach 80% market share.
  • Your program doesn't provide an input.

  Game input

Input for a turn

Line 1 : 10 integers id, playerCount, turn, market, cash, devs, sellers, managers, features, tests and bugs where:

  • id : your player id
  • playerCount : number of players
  • turn : number of turn since the beginning
  • market : market share in thousandth of your start-up, this value represents also your income
  • cash : cash of your start-up
  • devs : developers employees of your start-up
  • sellers : sellers employees of your start-up
  • managers : managers employees of your start-up
  • features : features developed in your software
  • tests : tests developed in your software
  • bugs : bugs in your software

playerCount following lines : one line for each player. First line is for player 0, next one for player 1, etc. Each line contains 3 integers startUpId id of the start-up, marketShare market share of the start-up and employeesCount total employees of the start-up

Output for a turn
One line containing 5 integers separated by space devsToHire, sellersToHire, managersToHire, debugDevs and aggressiveSellers

Constraints for output values:

  • -1 ≤ managersToHire ≤ 1
  • |devsToHire| + |sellersToHire| ≤ 2 × managers where |n| means absolute value of n
  • 0 ≤ debugDevs ≤ devs + devsToHire
  • 0 ≤ aggressiveSellers ≤ sellers + sellersToHire
  • devs + devsToHire + sales + salesToHire ≤ 10 × managers
devsToHire is the number of developers you want to hire for next round, this number can be negative.
sellersToHire is the number of sellers you want to hire for next round, this number can be negative.
managersToHire is the number of managers you want to hire for next round, this number can be negative.
debugDevs is the number of your developers that will be affected to fixe bugs. By fixing a bug it will reduce the bugs number by 1 and increase tests number by 1. If there is no bug in your software it will just increase tests number by 1.
Beware tests number can be increased only if a feature already exists or has been developed during the turn
aggressiveSellers is the number of your sellers that will try to steal market share from other start-up. Note that it is harder to steal market than take it from free market.
